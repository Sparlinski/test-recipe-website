Coconut Beef Curry



This creamy delicous curry is easy and simple to make. Curry powders are wide and varied in the dynamics they bring to a dish. If you have access to a local indian market, I highly recommend picking up a powder that you can get there, it will not only enhance the flavor of the dish but you can also bring a level of heat to the dish that you cannot get from standard store bought curry powders. Regardless, the spices and flavors that are combined here will be unleashed regardless of the powder you choose.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of cubed steak
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 pound of sweet potato
  • 1 cup of sliced mushrooms
  • 1/4 tablespoon of cinnamon powder
  • 1 diced onion
  • 1 sprig of curry leaves
  • 3 cloves of diced garlic
  • tablespoon of ginger
  • 3 tablespoons of curry powder
  • half a can of coconut milk
  • 1 cup of beef broth
  • salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 1 tablspoon of flour
  • half tablespoon of coconut sugar

Cooking Instructions

  1. Dice onion and garlic, add oil to a pot and place and medium heat, fry onion and garlic while meat is being prepped
  2. Cut beef into approx 2cm cubes, add to pot when ready
  3. Add 3 tablespoons of curry powder to pot, stir meat, onions and garlic until evenly coated with curry powder. Fry until meat is browned
  4. While meat is browning, peel and cut sweet potato, and wash and slice mushrooms
  5. Once meat is browned, add coconut milk, cinnamon, ginger, beef broth to pot and stir until Ingredients are mixed
  6. Add bay leaves, curry leaves, salt lemon juice, and coconut sugar to the pot. Stir, add mushrooms, stir again and bring to boil
  7. Reduce to a low medium temperature, cover pot and let simmer for 15mins
  8. After 15mins, remove cover, add sweet potatoes and replace cover
  9. Let simmer for another 15 mins
  10. Place tablespoon of flour to a cup, mix with a little water to make a creamy texture. Add flour to pot and stir thourally
  11. Leave cover of pot, reduce to a low heat and cook for 10 more minutes. Stir occasionaly to prevent flour from coagualating or burning bottom of the pot.
  12. Serve curry with naan bread, or your choice of rice
  13. Chutney and natural yogurt are very good served with this curry recipe
